Prologue - Kieran

She had no idea she was being hunted.

I watched her through the window of the cafe. The same cafe she visited every Wednesday and Friday between her investigative journalism class and her evening shift at the campus library. Consistent and predictable. As always.

She tucked a strand of her auburn hair behind her ear, those Verdant eyes fixed on her laptop screen with that intense concentrated look that I had come to love over the past Five years.

Five whole years.

In those years I had been observing and learning everything I possibly could about Sienna Clarke while she remained blissfully unaware about my existence. She did not remember me—but I did. Now I knew everything there was about her.

To her, I was just a stranger. But for me—it was more than that. Because I could remember. I could remember everything. She had saved my life once. Exactly five years from today. She had no idea the man she had helped outside of a convenience store—the one bleeding out in an alley after a dispute between powerful families—was much more than a stranger she had simply called 911 for before disappearing.

But I remembered—everything.

Her voice, steady despite the fear of the situation. The way she had firmly pressed her khaki field jacket against my wound without an ounce of hesitation in those grey-green eyes. The streetlight reflected in them, revealing concern for a man she had not even known.

She had been 18. Barely. A college freshman with her whole life and a bright future ahead of her.

And from the moment she had touched me I had been lost. Completely and utterly undone.

At that moment I realized after she left that I needed to find her. I needed to know everything about her. I wanted her. I had become obsessed—No—

I was in love.

I had fallen in love with Sienna Clarke.

And this cruel world did not deserve her. They did not appreciate her as I did. And so I made my decision. Now it was not just about watching her—it was about keeping her.

She left the cafe three minutes early—as always. I entered at the time she had made her leave. And her shoulders had brushed against mine. It was—overwhelming, to say the least. I felt the denim of her jacket glide against the fabric of my suit. She was wearing a campus shirt underneath—Her 10th time wearing it this year. It only made the anticipation harder to endure.

I adjusted my cufflinks while requesting an espresso from the woman at the counter. Plain, no milk, and the faintest drop of cream. The espresso lightens by shade. Nothing more. Just enough to take the edge off.

After I finished my espresso, I sat—and waited. Anxiously. Patience was never my strong suit, but after half a decade of watching, yearning—I would finally bring her home. Where she would be safe, loved, and more importantly—she would be appreciated.

And then came the first signal.

My driver was already waiting outside her apartment building. And all that was left was for me to take action. Everything had already been prepared—the room, her wardrobe, and everything else she would ever need. And more.

I had made it to her apartment in no time. She had not gone inside yet. She never did when she got home. She would always water her plants in the exact same order. Her consistency made her easier to read. She would start with the Alocasia—then the Aloe, and Anthurium. Routine. Expected.

It had only been five minutes since I received the second signal. But it felt like ages. That did not matter now. If this was going to go smoothly I had to maintain my composure.

And so I did.

I approached her. Carefully. Quietly. She had been unaware that at this very moment, she was prey. The street was empty. The sun was setting. The only sound present was that of shrieking Cicadas.

I was close enough that I could hear her breathing. Slow. Gentle. Steady.

Close enough that it would be impossible for her to outrun the inevitable.

The sun dipped below the buildings.

And then—

My fingers pressed a silk handkerchief to her face. It happened so quickly she had no time to react. She gasped out of surprise—which only made going unconscious happen faster than I anticipated. She tried to grip my arms, to fight back using the last of the strength her body would allow her. But there was no use.

She was already mine.

Her eyes rolled back and her knees buckled. She was practically deadweight. As I steadied her with one hand on her cheek, the other went to her waist for balance. She mumbled through the fabric of the cloth. Her arms began to go limp.

“Do not be afraid, Sienna. I will not hurt you. You are safe now” I embraced her. Her body was warm. Breathing faint. Her hair—she was wearing her favorite peach-scented shampoo again. And the strands felt like smooth cream against my skin.

I effortlessly lifted her into my arms. I cradled her close—the proximity felt unreal. Her head fell against my shoulder—it was like she fit against me perfectly.

I carefully placed her in the backseat of the SUV. I already had soft pillows and warm blankets inside—wrapping her slowly. Carefully.

I could see Felix—my driver—sneak a glimpse through the rear view mirror.

“With all due respect, sir—is that really necessary?”

“She deserves comfort even in captivity”, I replied, placing a hand on her head.

“Now drive.”

And he did.

Despite the low hum of the radio, the only sound I could focus on was her breathing.

Despite the beautiful view outside, she had my full attention. This was really happening. We would finally be together. Happy.

I brushed my thumb along her cheek.

“It is time to go home.”
